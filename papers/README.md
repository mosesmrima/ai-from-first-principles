## Suggested reading order

Foundational LLM lineage — read roughly in this order:

1. **Attention Is All You Need** — Vaswani et al. (2017) — the transformer
2. **GPT-1** — Radford et al. (2018) — generative pretraining
3. **BERT** — Devlin et al. (2018) — bidirectional encoders
4. **GPT-2** — Radford et al. (2019) — scale + zero-shot
5. **GPT-3** — Brown et al. (2020) — in-context learning
6. **InstructGPT / RLHF** — Ouyang et al. (2022) — alignment via human feedback
7. **Chinchilla** — Hoffmann et al. (2022) — scaling laws
8. **LLaMA 1 & 2** — Touvron et al. (2023) — efficient open models
9. **FlashAttention** — Dao et al. (2022) — IO-aware attention
10. **DPO** — Rafailov et al. (2023) — preference optimization without RL
11. **Toolformer** — Schick et al. (2023) — models that call tools
12. **ReAct** — Yao et al. (2022) — reasoning + acting
13. **Constitutional AI** — Bai et al. (2022) — AI feedback for alignment

## How to read a paper (3-pass method)

1. **Pass 1 (5 min):** title, abstract, intro, section headings, conclusion. Decide if it's worth more.
2. **Pass 2 (1 h):** figures, results, key equations. Note what's claimed and what's assumed. Skip proofs.
3. **Pass 3 (deep):** reconstruct it — could you reimplement the core idea? This is the pass that counts.

Then write the one-pager. Monthly: reproduce one result in code.
